# about

i'm director of engineering at sage, where i focus on ai enablement and innovation. i'm based in bangalore, india, and lately i feel like a recent victim of [token anxiety](https://writing.nikunjk.com/p/token-anxiety). my parents think i work in a call center. honestly, on some days they're not entirely wrong.

i joined sage through the lockstep/finly acquisition, where i led engineering and product. i wasn't sure at first how long i'd stay. what changed it was the people: colleagues i trust, who care about each other, the business, and the quality of what they build. that combo matters to me.

i'm on a mission to make the organisation and teams ai-native-not just engineering, but product, operations, and every function that touches the work. i'm especially interested in [service as software](https://www.uxtigers.com/post/service-as-software), which i think is the next definition of saas: productising what used to be purely human-delivered services. i currently operate more on the application layer, but i'm closely watching the models and the underlying infrastructure-it's not very far from hardware, and that frontier moves fast.

part of this mission comes from my own interest. i've built 30+ personal projects in the last twelve months, i read an unreasonable amount of content-papers, blogs, videos, rabbit holes-and it never ends. that's actually why i started putting links and recommendations in one place. i was spamming people. it wasn't healthy. most of the building has been for myself so far, but i'm starting to build in public now. this site is part of that.

my story hasn't been a straight line. i got into medical school and chose engineering instead. i prepared for the civil services (wanted to be a diplomat), studied everything from biology to economics and public administration, and didn't clear the exam. later i co-founded a startup backed by antler with a stellar co-founder from the united states. in about a year she had to return and that chapter closed, but it gave me a first-hand sense of what it means to run a business. as cto and co-founder i learned that building software is only part of the job; the rest is customers, operations, trade-offs, and responsibility.

that journey clarified what i'm drawn to. i moved from healthcare into accounting and b2b saas. i like problems that are meaningful, complex, and durable, and environments where product, engineering, and business value are wired together. i like people who care what they're building and how well it's built.

i've been drawn to math and statistics since college. i topped a lot of courses but the one i really fell in love with was pattern recognition in 2011, and that pull toward ml and ai never left. i like understanding systems and making them useful. i also like automating things-my own life and sometimes other people's-and i build projects on weekends. i'd like those to grow into something meaningful over time, but i also expect to keep working; work gives my life a kind of meaning and growth that purely solo building doesn't. i like that balance.

## what i value

- trust. i trust few people, but when i do, i show up fully.
- intellectual honesty. people who say what they mean and don't hide behind performance.
- loyalty. mutual commitment, especially in teams.
- craft with purpose. work that's well-made and actually useful to the business.
- learning. i'm a constant learner-tech, philosophy, reading, experience.
- depth over noise. substance over posturing, long-term over short-term optics.

## outside work

reading has probably shaped me the most. i once had over a thousand books and had read most of them before i gave them up and moved online. i still love physical copies but one day i decided i didn't want that much space taken by objects. these days i read mostly non-fiction and philosophy, with a soft spot for classic fiction.

i also live in a few lanes: building, movement, ideas. weekends = projects. i've done some music theory and want to go deeper into music and writing when life allows. i stay sane through movement-badminton, cycling, lately hiking. i don't travel a lot, but when i do i'm drawn to history, architecture, and how ideas shape a place.

i grew up in sri ganganagar in rajasthan, india, in a family of teachers. that upbringing still shows: i care about learning, substance, and steady effort. however messy the path has been, those things stuck.
